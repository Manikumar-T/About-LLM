# Fine-Tuning

## What is it?
**Fine-tuning** is continuing the training of a pre-trained model on a smaller, task-specific dataset to adapt its behaviour — without training from scratch.

## Why does it matter?
- Pre-trained LLMs are general-purpose. Fine-tuning makes them specialists.
- Much cheaper than pre-training (hours vs months of compute).
- Produces more reliable, on-topic outputs than prompt engineering alone.

## Types of fine-tuning

### 1. Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)
Train on `(prompt, completion)` pairs using cross-entropy loss.
```
### Instruction:
Translate to French: "Good morning"
### Response:
Bonjour
```

### 2. RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)
- Collect human preference rankings of model outputs.
- Train a **reward model** on those rankings.
- Fine-tune the LLM with PPO to maximise the reward.
- Used by ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini to improve helpfulness and safety.

### 3. DPO (Direct Preference Optimisation)
Simplification of RLHF — trains directly on preference pairs without a separate reward model. Faster and more stable.

## Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT)

Full fine-tuning updates all billions of parameters — very expensive. PEFT methods update only a tiny fraction:

### LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation)
Freeze the original weight matrix **W** and add a low-rank update:
```
W' = W + (α/r) × B × A
```
- **r** = rank (4–64). Smaller = fewer trainable params.
- **A** and **B** are the only trained matrices (~1% of total params).
- At inference, merge B×A into W — zero added latency.

### QLoRA
LoRA applied on top of a **4-bit quantised** model. Enables fine-tuning a 70B model on a single consumer GPU.

| Factual knowledge injection | 1,000 – 10,000 |
